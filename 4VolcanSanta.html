Santa Ana Volcano Lamatepec, El Salvador

The Santa Ana volcano or also known as the Lamatepec volcano is the highest volcano in El Salvador with an elevation of 2,381m, its last eruptions occurred in 1904, 1920, and 2005. Its geological history dates back to the Pleistocene era, between 22 and 1 million years ago, it is the least difficult to climb, although a suitable condition will help you have an easier ascent. The Santa Ana volcano tour lasts approximately 4 hours. 2 hours to go up and 2 hours to go down. Once you have reached the top of the Santa Ana volcano, you will be amazed by the beautiful turquoise lagoon that sits at the bottom of the crater. From the crater, the landscapes are simply indescribable. You can see the splendor of the Pacific Ocean, the Izalco volcano, and the tranquility of Lake Coatepeque. You should come to this volcano to enjoy the best things of El Salvador.
