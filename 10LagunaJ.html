Laguna del Jocotal El Tránsito, El Salvador

The perfect lagoon

The beautiful Jocotal lagoon located in El Transito, El Salvador, is distinguished by the richness of its biodiversity, for example in 1976 as a protected natural area. In 1983 it was declared a "bird sanctuary" Birds are typical in the lagoon: snail hawk, white pelican, zarceta, water cock, duck. The Lagoon is basically a depression, formed by groundwater from the lava mantle of the Chaparrastique volcano, it has two drainages, one natural and one artificial, both drain towards the Rio Grande de San Miguel. It also serves as a flood controller, for fixing of carbon and climate regulation, wood is produced, agriculture is exploited, there is fishing and it has tourism potential. In this destination you will be able to observe mangroves, dry forests, alligators, crocodiles and a great variety of birds and fish; or you can practice kayaking, artisanal fishing or have an adventure in a canoe with paddles.
