Civil war in El Salvador (Oct 15, 1979 – Jan 16, 1992)

The Salvador Civil War was a twelve year time of civil war in El Salvador that was between the government of El Salvador and the Farabundo Martí National Liberation Front or FMNL. The number of victims of this civil war is about 75,000 dead and 15,000 missing. The war finished after a process of peace between the parties with the signing of the Peace in Mexico on January 16 of 1992. This made the demobilization of the rebels and their incorporation into the political life of the country.

The civil war in El Salvador has been considered as one of the conflicts derived from the Cold War. The two sides in conflict in El Salv ador civil war were influenced by the global conflict. The Government of El Salvador had maintained a firm alliance with the United States since the mid-twentieth century. While the FMLN maintained relations with the countries of the Eastern European socialist bloc, Cuba and Nicaragua but only Nicaragua supported the FMLN, giving them weapons until 1982.

This conflict changed all the history of Salvador being one of the most important events of the country.
