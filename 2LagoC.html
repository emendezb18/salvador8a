Lago coatepeque (Santa Ana, El Salvador)

The turquoise lake

Coatepeque lake is important in El Salvador because over the years it has changed from dark blue to turquoise, always happens in the months of September, October and November, and it has happened 6 times in the last 25 years. Scientists don’t know why this color changes, but some hypothesis say that it is because some types of bacteria change the color of the lake. Pipiles:descendants of the nahuas that emigrated from Mexico in pre-columbian times have been in El Salvador in the central and oriental part of El Salvador, they know about the color change, so they made a temple and monolith on the island “anteojos” and “isla de cerro” where Pipiles adore the god Itzcueye. It’s important to El Salvador because many people used it to do fishing and also have water in their homestead. It also participated in a competition of the 8 wonders of the world, it didn’t win but it got second place.
This place attracts a lot of tourists and has many hotels in the nearby areas, there are about 5 thousand visitors per month, and surrounding the lake live about 20,000 residents. If you want to go there you should catch a flight to the city of Santa Ana and drive by car 27.3 km or about 1 hour to the lake. When you visit it you would enjoy different activities like jet skiing and kayaking. The temperature is usually between 19 and 29 degrees Celsius and has a tropical climate. The best months to visit it are April to July where the temperature is minimum 20 to maximum 31. Don’t wait to go to this unique and beautiful lake.
