Lake Ilopango San Salvador, El salvador

On the edge of San Salvador we can see Lake Ilopango, which was the scene of one of the most horrible natural disasters in history more than 1500 years ago. The Ilopango area volcano erupted, covering the region in ash and destroying the Mayan villages there. The volcanic caldera, which was created by four violent eruptions between 300,000 and 90,000 years ago, is currently the location of 17 newer volcanoes. This lake has 72 meters of distance. Lake Ilopango is very similar in size and appearance to Crater Lake in Oregon, USA, and, as does Crater Lake, it has an island. The big difference is that Lake Ilopango is a popular resort area and is surrounded by mountain homes to take advantage of it's cooler climate. Lake Ilopango, a crater lake, fills a scenic volcanic caldera in central El Salvador, not far from San Salvador.At the moment the entrance to the lake is free
