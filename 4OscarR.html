Murder of Oscar Romero (March 24 of 1980)

The murder of “monseñor Romero”

Oscar Romero or more known as monseñor Romero was archbishop of El Salvador who sacrificed his life to give help to people with low resources, he also protested to the government of El Salvador because of the civil war that was killing thousands of Salvadorans. The murder of monseñor Romero was Marino Samayor Acosta, sergeant of section 2 of the former National Guard and member of the security team of the president of El Salvador, the order was given from Major Roberto d'Aubuisson, creator of the squads of the death and founder of ARENA (alianza republicana nacionalista) and of Colonel Arturo Armando Molina give Marino Samayor Acosta 114 dollars for making the action. Monseñor Romero is one of the most important persons of El Salvador because he is a sign of protest and opportunity in the country.
