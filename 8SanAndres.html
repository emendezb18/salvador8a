San Andrés (La libertad)

The capital of the mayan empire in El Salvador

San Andrés is a pre-Columbian site in El Salvador, whose occupation began around the year 900 BC in the department of La Libertad. This place was evacuated in the year 250 because of the eruption of the caldera of Lago Ilopango, and was occupied again in the 5th Century with many other sites in the valley of Zapotitán. Between 600 and 900 AD San Andrés was the capital of a Mayan empire in the valle de Zapotitan with control over the other places where Mayans live in this part of El Salvador.

If you want to go you should take a flight to El Salvador and drive 30.7 km or 1 hour. You should go to see the history and architecture of this ancient place constructed and havitaded by Mayan civilization.
