Independence of El Salvador (1821)

The heroes of the independence

The first independent trial in El Salvador was in San Salvador in November 5 of 1811 when “José Matías Delgado” make sound the bell of the church to say all of the people to be ready to start the independence movement, some historians say that this not happened but it’s the cultural story that salvadorans say, but this movement was detained by the Spanish soldiers in December of the same year Second and third independence movement. In the second independence movement Manuel José Arce and Manuel Rodríguez were the main motivators of the independence movement, this one happened 3 years after the first independence movement but this one also failed, the independence for El Salvador come 1 decade after the second independence movement, the independence act was signed by José Matías Delgado in September 15 of 1821. El Salvador and the other provinces of Central America proclaimed their independence from Spain in 1821. Under Gen. Manuel Jose Arce, the five Central American nations united to become the United Provinces of Central America in 1823. El Salvador declared itself to be an independent republic in 1839, despite the fact that the following several decades saw numerous revolutions. Normal ties between the United States and El Salvador were broken after a coup in 1931. The Salvadoran government was assisted by the US against socialist forces in the 1980s. The independence movement was led by prominent Salvadoran and Central American figures such as José Matías Delgado, Manuel José Arce, and Santiago José Celis.
