Gulf of Fonseca La Union, El Salvador

Bordering El Salvador, Honduras, and Nicaragua we can see the beautiful Gulf of Fonseca, one of the best natural harbors in the world, with an area of about 3,200 km². Its important ports include La Unión, in El Salvador; San Lorenzo, in Honduras, Potosí and Puerto Morazán, in Nicaragua. Among the most important islands of the Gulf of Fonseca stand out: Meanguera, Conchagüita, Zacatillo, Conchagua and Martín Pérez, belonging to El Salvador. The three countries Honduras, El Salvador and Nicaragua with the coastline along the gulf ended up involved in a conflict over the rights to the gulf and its islands. In 1992, a resolution of the International Court of Justice in The Hague decided on the land, the islands and the maritime conflict on the borders, a partition. In its ruling, the International Court of Justice determined that El Salvador, Honduras and Nicaragua would share control of the Gulf of Fonseca.
